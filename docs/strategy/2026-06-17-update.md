# 2026-06-17 strategy update — what shifted in the 5 days since 06-12

> **Audience:** anyone reading [`ROADMAP.md`](../../ROADMAP.md), [`docs/strategy/2026-06-competitiveness.md`](2026-06-competitiveness.md), or [`docs/compare.md`](../compare.md) who wants to know *why* the framing was sharpened on 2026-06-17.
>
> **TL;DR:** five days, five external shifts, and one in-house experimental campaign. The cumulative effect: code-mode is no longer a differentiator (it became table stakes); governance + isolation became the strongest position we hadn't been pitching; and our `evals-runner` paired-statistics harness got a worked-example ammunition crate it didn't have before.

---

## 0. Why bother updating in 5 days

Roadmaps that don't keep pace with the field rot fast in 2026 — every quarter brings a non-trivial reshuffle of who has what. The 06-12 strategy memo was sharp at the time (in-tree fact-checking confirmed every "shipped" claim). It just didn't survive the 06-12 → 06-17 news cycle without sanding.

This update is **not a rewrite**. It's a delta on top of the 06-12 axes (S1–S4), driven by:

1. A high-quality external strategic review (referenced in §1).
2. Our own `arm-f vs bare` and `arm-batch-grammar` ablation campaign on 06-17 (referenced in §2 and §3).

## 2. The internal experimental campaign (06-17, same day)

Independently of the external review, we ran three rounds of in-tree ablation against `evomerge-t10-1b7-v7f:latest` (a Qwen3-1.7B LoRA produced by the evomerge consumer of agentkit-js, target G1 ≥56% on multi-turn tool execution arm-f). Reports under [`docs/reports/`](../reports/).

| Round | Headline result | What it falsified or confirmed |
|-------|-----------------|--------------------------------|
| **1** — `arm-f` (param-only Pick/Provide) vs `bare` (no grammar) | arm-f **41.1%** [31.5, 51.4] vs bare **12.2%** [7.0, 20.6]; McNemar p = **2.56 × 10⁻⁶** | **Confirmed** grammar-pinned tool calling is a structural lift on small models (+28.9 pp). Killed the in-house assumption that arm-f "constrains too much." |
| **2** — added `arm-batch-grammar` (full plan in one call, json-schema-strict) | batch-grammar **14.4%** [8.6, 23.2] **<<** arm-f **38.9%**. McNemar p = 0.016 vs bare. | **Falsified** the post-round-1 hypothesis that *"Pick/Provide loses global plan view, so giving the model the entire plan upfront should recover the gap."* Pick/Provide is an asset, not a cost; small models *cannot use* the upfront-plan capability even when given it. |
| **3** — sanity recheck on round 1 | bare round-2 = 6.7% (vs round-1 12.2%); the **bare-wins=3** cells from round 1 (cart-3step-add-remove ×2, cal-4step-batch-create ×1) **did not reproduce**. | The hypothesis we'd been about to feed evomerge as a training data recommendation rested on 3 sampling-noise cells. Caught only because we re-ran with paired McNemar against the same item set. |

The campaign also tried to baseline Qwen3-8B Q3_K_M on the same harness; on a high-end Apple Silicon MacBook Pro with Metal full-offload, **22 minutes elapsed before the first ablation cell completed**. Implication: the 8B baseline is ~50–100× slower than 1.7B on multi-step agent tasks (each task = 15+ LLM calls), and the upgrade-to-8B option for evomerge is ruled out by user-hardware reach (this MBP is among the strongest consumer hardware; users on lower-spec gear cannot run it at all).

Outcome handed to the evomerge team: not "increase training data" or "add plan-token SFT" (both falsified), but *"G1 = 56% is product-decision territory now: lower the threshold, redesign the task set, or accept the 1.7B ceiling."* See [`docs/strategy/recommendations-for-evomerge-2026-06-17.md`](recommendations-for-evomerge-2026-06-17.md).

## 3. The cross-product: what changed in our axes

Each axis below has a **before** (06-12) and **after** (06-17), with the source signal that drove the change.

### S1 — *embedded runtime* line, now sharpened

| | Before (06-12) | After (06-17) |
|---|----------------|---------------|
| Pitch | "Stop competing as a framework. Get embedded as a runtime — code-mode is the lever, agentkit's three-tier kernel matrix exists, sell to framework leaders." | Same direction, **tighter reason**: *not* "they need a sandbox" (they have one — OpenAI 2026-04). The reason is *they need the neutral, portable, multi-tier kernel that drops into their existing executor socket without locking them to a single vendor.* |
| Mechanism | "Sell as a component." | "Be a drop-in **executor** for Cloudflare codemode `DynamicWorkerExecutor`, OpenAI Agents SDK sandbox provider, Mastra sandbox provider. Swap the executor, keep your framework." |
| Signal that justifies it | code-mode token savings (≤14% at N=30) | code-mode is **table stakes**; the moat is *portability × multi-language × multi-tier × policy-uniform*, none of which a single-vendor sandbox provides. |

### S1' — *governance + isolation* line (★ added 06-17)

This is a **new axis**, not a refinement. Justification:

- `CapabilityManifest` is already shipped, deterministic, framework-neutral, runtime-enforced.
- Microsoft Agent Governance Toolkit is policy-only (no isolation).
- OWASP Agentic Top 10 + Colorado AI Act + EU AI Act + ISO 42001 = the procurement gate enterprises cite (75% of large orgs per KPMG 2026).
- Protocol layer (MCP / A2A) leaves authorization to the implementation.

Concrete artifacts: [`docs/security/capability-manifest-owasp.md`](../security/capability-manifest-owasp.md), updated [`SECURITY.md`](../../SECURITY.md). Posture: *complementary to* MS toolkit, not competing.

### S2 — *referee, not contestant*

| | Before (06-12) | After (06-17) |
|---|----------------|---------------|
| Pitch | "Replace self-built numbers with public-leaderboard numbers (LongMemEval-500 first, SWE-bench-lite second)." | Same primitives, **inverted positioning**: *paired statistical methodology* is the asset, not a single benchmark headline. Position `evals-runner` as the *referee*. Pursue **BEAM (ICLR 2026)** as additional target — it's the new frontier and (for now) untainted. |
| Worked example | Bundled 6-item LongMemEval fixture as smoke test | The 06-17 three-round arm-f vs bare vs batch-grammar campaign is now the public worked example. Round 2 falsifying round 1's hypothesis demonstrates exactly what the harness exists to do. |

### S3 — *zero-deploy local Studio*

Smaller change. Vercel AI SDK 6 (2026-Q1) shipped a DevTools panel with 20M+ monthly downloads behind it, narrowing our differentiator. Surviving angle: *no hosting, no account, no telemetry phone-home.* Still real, but not the headline.

### S4 — *bscode is a funnel, not a product*

Direction unchanged. **Demo content updated**: headline scene is now `CapabilityManifest`-blocks-an-OWASP-attack live demo, not "another coding agent." This serves S1' and aligns with the bscode README's existing "What this demonstrates" framing.

### SL-4 — *trust + protocol alignment*

Holdovers from the external review:

- **Bus factor remains the master gate.** Single-maintainer publishing on npm cancels enterprise procurement before any technical evaluation. No engineering work fixes this — only adding a co-publisher does.
- **1.0 freeze face.** The 06-12 `api-stability.md` already enumerates the SemVer-covered surface; cutting an actual 1.0 tag with a public freeze statement matters more for adoption than any new feature in the next 90 days.
- **MCP 2026-06 spec conformance tests.** Add to `mcp-server` in Q3.

## 4. What we will NOT change (anti-drift checklist)

Equally important: which 06-12 commitments survived the update untouched. Listing them so future updates don't quietly drift away from them.

1. **agentkit-js stays a generic foundation.** Product concepts (bscode, evomerge, customer task sets) live in the consumer repos. *No* product-specific code lands in `packages/`. Confirmed by 06-17 experimental campaign — every new arm (batch-grammar) and new statistic landed in the generic `evals-runner`, not in evomerge or bscode.
2. **No SaaS, no telemetry phone-home.** Local-first, BYO-key.
3. **Public CHANGELOG, double-week release cadence, public security disclosure SLA.** Documented in [`SECURITY.md`](../../SECURITY.md).
4. **Synthetic fixtures with no train-set contamination.** This is what makes `evals-runner` a referee at all; it's the methodological contract.
5. **bscode is funnel-only, never the lead product.** README, `/recipes`, headline demo all serve `npm add @agentkit-js/core` traffic.

## 6. What would invalidate this update

Defaulting to the 06-12 memo's spirit: *write down what would change our minds.*

1. **If governance-conscious enterprises don't actually pay attention to the OWASP / EU-AI-Act / ISO-42001 mapping**, S1' degrades from "★ new line" back to "compliance documentation hygiene." Observation signal: do the OWASP/regulatory references cited in [`docs/security/capability-manifest-owasp.md`](../security/capability-manifest-owasp.md) appear in any inbound enterprise procurement question by 2026-Q4? Empty inbox = signal we overestimated this axis.
2. **If MS Agent Governance Toolkit ships native isolation in 2026-H2**, S1's "real WASM isolation" advantage shrinks. Mitigation: stay positioned as their *runtime layer* (called by them), not as a competitor.
3. **If BEAM gets quickly "occupied" by a single high-profile vendor's score**, the "first paired-statistics BEAM run" window narrows. Mitigation: the position is *methodology*, not the score; a single vendor's headline doesn't take the methodology slot.
4. **If single-maintainer publish status persists past 2026-Q3**, every other axis is irrelevant (procurement filters out before reaching them).

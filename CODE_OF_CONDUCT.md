# Contributor Code of Conduct

WasmAgent is an open-source project. To keep collaboration productive
for everyone — contributors, reviewers, users — we ask all participants
to follow a few simple norms.

## Expected behaviour

- **Be respectful.** Treat other contributors as colleagues. Disagree
  with ideas; do not attack people.
- **Be specific.** When raising a concern, point at code, logs, or
  measurable behaviour. "This is bad" without context wastes
  everyone's time.
- **Be patient.** Maintainers are volunteers. Reviews can take days.
  Bumping a thread once is fine; repeated pings are not.
- **Assume the best interpretation.** If a message could be read as
  rude or as a misunderstanding, treat it as a misunderstanding and
  ask a clarifying question.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Personal attacks, derogatory comments, sustained disruption of
  discussion threads, or any conduct directed at a contributor's
  identity rather than their work.
- Sharing other contributors' private information (real-world
  identity, email, private chat content) without explicit consent.
- Anything illegal under the laws of the contributor's jurisdiction.

## Scope

This document applies to all project spaces: GitHub repositories
(issues, PRs, discussions, commit messages, code comments), any
official chat channel, public talks or social-media posts that
explicitly represent the project.

## Reporting

If you see behaviour that violates the norms above, or you feel
uncomfortable participating, please open a private issue in
`wasmagent-js` and add the label `governance`, or email the address
listed in the repo's `CITATION.cff` if one exists. Maintainers will
treat reports as confidential and respond within seven days.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may, at their discretion:

1. Reply privately with a clarification request,
2. Reply publicly with a moderation note and ask for the behaviour
   to stop,
3. Temporarily restrict a contributor's ability to comment or open
   PRs in the project's repositories,
4. Permanently remove the contributor from the project's GitHub
   organization.

Steps 3 and 4 require agreement from at least two maintainers.

## Attribution

This document is loosely modelled on the
[Contributor Covenant 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/),
adapted to the smaller, technical scope of this project. The
substantive expectations match; the wording is our own.

## Scope across the WasmAgent ecosystem

The same expectations apply to all three WasmAgent open-source
repositories — `wasmagent-js`, `bscode`, `trace-pipeline`. The
sibling repos' `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md` files just point here.
